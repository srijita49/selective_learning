In this experiment you will be given with a {{Constants.num_rows}} by {{Constants.num_rows}} matrix. Each cell of the matrix would generate a payoff of 1 or 0. You can open as many cells as you like to learn about the payoff in the cell. Also you can choose to see the total number of 1s in a row or a column by opening cells in the average row or column.

Information Stage:

For each round a random number between {{Constants.low_limit}} and {{Constants.high_limit}} would be generated. This value would be called the cutoff value If you choose to open a cell you will see a problem of the following form,

0.23141552+0.24661835-0.23638687.

If the result of this expression is higher than the cutoff value then the payoff from the cell is 1 otherwise the payoff is zero.

If you choose to open a cell in the average row or column then you will a problem of follwing type:

The second last digit represents the number of ones in the row:

.51217116.

Choice Stage:

Once you have are ready to choose a cell, you need to continue to the choice stage. In the choice stage you need to choose a row and a column. If you choose cell (i,j), row i and column j then you'll earn the payoff from cell (i,j).

You would also be allowed to choose the average row or column. For example, if you choose average column and second row, then one cell would be chosen randomly from second row and your payoff would be the payoff of the randomly chosen cell.

However, if you choose both average row and average column together then your payoff would be zero.

You will be five choices in the choice stage. Only one choice at random will be chosen for payoff. You can also choose the exact same cell for five times as well.

In the following few pages we will ask questions about the structure of the experiment. Once you are done with the quiz go to the actual experiment. You will have 30 minutes to finish as many rounds of the experiment as possible. Your final payoff would be sum of payoff from all the rounds.

In the next few pages you will asked some questions about the payoff structure. You have answer atleast 4 correct answers to continue to the actual experiment.
